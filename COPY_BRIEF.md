## SECTION 2 — THE BOTTLENECK (PROBLEM)

**Section headline (verbatim from brief §4):**
> Most High-Volume Vendors Are Built for Volume. Your Prototype Isn't.

**Subheadline (pain framing):**
> You're building a medical device, not a consumer widget. The CAD file is right. The bench test is scheduled. And a 6-week lead time from a production-scale shop means your investor meeting happens before your prototype does — or after your FDA window closes.

**Three problem beats (one per objection category from Q8):**

1. **Tolerance and delamination.** A high-volume production setup treats your STL/STEP file like a cosmetic print job. Layer adhesion, post-processing, and material selection matter when you need bench-tested results — not cosmetic.

2. **NDA and IP.** A high-volume production vendor running tier-one OEM programs doesn't blink at your file. A prototype shop that handles inventor IP with NDA-ready processes, design controls, and short-run discipline does.

3. **Scaling to pilot production.** Most shops can quote you a one-off. Few can carry you from functional prototype through pilot production on the same equipment, same materials, same engineers — without a no second vendor between iterations.
